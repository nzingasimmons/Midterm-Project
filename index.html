black net.art repository

_digital culture is increasingly ubiquitous. algorithmically driven digital spaces are slowly collapsing the divide between the real world and the virtual worlds housed behind a screen.

_for black bodies in particular, the digital realm presents a conundrum. Simultaneously invisible and hyper visible, blackness on the internet is consumed with virality in the form of popularized memes and GIFs, and more violently in images of police brutality amplified on social media.

_however, despite the proliferation of black bodies in cyberspace, those same bodies often go unconsidered in the construction of algorithmically driven digital technologies. in safiya noble's "algorithms of oppression" she writes, "although rhetoric of the information age broadly seeks to minimize the hegemonic backdrop of the technological revolution, African Americans have embraced, modified, and contextualized technology into significantly different frameworks despite the relations of power expressed in the algorithms.‚Äù

_employing noble's assertions about the radical and inventive ways that black people engage the digital as a touchstone

_black net.art repository aims to provide a view of african american contemporary artists working in new media, and a collection of texts related to the topic.
