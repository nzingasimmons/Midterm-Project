artists





_a collection of artists who refuse the assumed neutrality of digital technologies and position the digital as a space uniquely primed for the abandonment of corporeality and the assertion of black futurity.



_e.jane (b.1990) is a conceptual new media and performance artist based in New York. Their interdisciplinary practice incorporates digital images, video, text, performance, sculpture, installation, and sound design. inspired by radical black liberation and womanist movements, e. jane’s work examines the ways in which black femme bodies navigate space in popular culture and digital media.

_caitlin cherry (b.1987) is a contemporary artist based in Chicago. Cherry practice incorporates psychedelic paintings and multimedia installations to examine both the hyper visualization and sexualization of black women on the Internet. Cherry’s vibrant kaleidoscopic abstract works depict rappers, singers, and exotic dancers that she finds images of on Instagram. Cherry employs an abstract visual language that protects the black women she paints from being legibly consumed in the same way they might in digital spaces like Instagram.

Qualesha Wood (b.1997) is a contemporary artist based in New York whose praxis merges the digital and the phyiscal through the employment of a traditional art form – weaving. Wood creates textiles that references the symbols and sayings of digital culture in wool.Wood redacts the pejoratively sexual representations of black women that exist in online spaces––such as Google’s search engine––and instead, inserts an authentic representation of black womanhood through self-imaging.

_shawne michaelain holloway (b. 1991), a new media artist based in chicago, explores the consumption of black femme bodies on the web. Within her praxis, she employs sound, video, and performance to examine the dialectic between technology and sexuality as tools for exposing and undermining hegemonic structures of power. holloway’s praxis disrupts the neoliberalist structure of the consumption of black bodies on the web.

_sondra perry (b.1986)is an interdisciplinary new media artist based in New Jersey. she employs video, 3-D animation, and performance to explore the abstraction of black identities in the digital realm. within her praxis, perry conceptualizes the digital as a liminal space uniquely primed for the critique of misrepresentations and the assertion of expansive black identities.

_jacobly satterwhite (b. 1986) is an interdisciplinary new media artist who employs performance, video, 3D animation and sculpture to explore themes of memory, desire and ritual in his work. intersted in artistic praxis as a metanarrative, satterwhite's work ruminates on the relationship between the past, present and future, and how those temporalities can communicate a broad, shared experience.

_juliana huxtable(b. 1987) is an interdisciplinary conceptual artist who emplosys a diverse set of mediums, including prints, performance, self-portraiture, and social media, to explore issues at the intersection of race, gender, queernes, and identity. Her work often utilzes her own body to examine the socio-political structures that impact the way marginalized groups navigate the world.

arthur jafa (b. 1960) is an conceptual artist and cinematographer. his contemporary practice, makes use of films, artifacts, and other digitally scoured media to ruminate on an ontology of universal blackness. through his praxis, jafa examines the question: "how can visual media transmit the equivalent power, beauty, and alienation embedded within US culture.
